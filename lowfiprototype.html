Prototypes

We grouped our ideas into 3 potential prototypes:

Prototype

Pros and Cons

Final Decision

1. Seating arrangement

Select your seat when you join the meeting. Whisper to only 1 person near you or multiple people in your group. Option to adjust volumes of the whisper room and the main speaker.

Pros:

  • Simulate reality where you’d be sitting next to someone and whispering to them
  • Cultivate teamwork and collaboration

Cons:

  • Extra step when joining the meeting
  • Unable to whisper to people not ‘sitting’ around you

Adopt

There are no alternatives out there that offer this solution so it would be interesting to test it out. It adheres to our goal of cultivating adhoc/sidebar and real time conversations.

2. Whispers as voice messages

Record and send whispers as voice messages to 1 or multiple people. Option to save or reply later.

Pros:

  • Less distracting because the receiver won’t be interrupted
  • Receiver can play the recording anytime

Cons:

  • Asynchronous
  • Users might prefer text ‘whispers’ over voice recording

Adopt

This is an interesting and less intrusive option. Voice whispers are more ‘personal’ compared to text whispers so it might enhance the video conferencing experience.

3. Whisper room

Direct ‘whisper’ or create a room with people you want to whisper to. Start a call on the side or whisper via text.

Pros:

  • Minimal changes from current video conferencing platforms

Cons:

  • Too similar to current solutions on the market

Discard

eZuce currently offers a similar feature. We think it’s better to experiment with new and distinct ideas.

 

Prototype #1 - Seating arrangement

In this prototype, users will choose a seat prior to joining the meeting. After that, they will be able to whisper to people in their group only. There is an option to control both the volume of the main speaker and your friend(s) engaged in a whisper. Users can also choose whether or not they want to auto-accept all incoming whispers, get prompted for a whisper or block all incoming whisper.

Prototype #2 - Whispers as voice messages

In this prototype, users can hold the ‘whisper 1:1’ button to record and release to send the message. When a reply has been received, users can play, pause, or save the recording. Users can also click on the ‘whisper 2+’ button to whisper to multiple people. A ‘whisper room’ will be created and more participants can be added, and users can send voice messages like how you would in current instant messaging software.

Usability Goals and Benchmark Tasks

Usability Goals

  1. Standard video conferencing tasks should feel familiar and comfortable for the user
  • The system design should not deviate too much from existing software
  1. The user can easily & quickly send neighbours whispers
  • Whispering is meant to be quick and easy. The system should be able to achieve this in order for whispers not to lose their meaning
  1. Initiating and engaging with whispers needs to be easy and seamless so the user can focus on the main speaker
  • Similar to real life situations, the system should allow users engaged in whispering to continue listening to the main speaker
  1. Navigating the application should not take away their attention from the main speaker
  • The system should be easy to use so that they can navigate without losing their focus
  1. The whisper functionality should be simple enough that the user requires little to no help from external sources
  • The system should not require complex manipulation that would require users to shift their focus to figuring out how it works with the help of external resources. This goes against whispers being quick and easy
  1. Satisfaction rating 3 or more during the post-test questionnaire
  • The system should bring value and enhance the video conferencing experience
